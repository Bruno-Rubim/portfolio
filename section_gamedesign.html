Perito dos E-mails (E-mail expert)

Note: The project and game are currently only avaliable in brazilian portuguese.
Due to issues in hosting the platform may have long loading times

This was part of my final project as a system development student at IFPR, an educational web game made with javascript to teach about malicious emails.

The game has the gameplay of Papers Please in the setting of an earlt 2000's computer, using parodies of real companies to simulate realistic scenarios about dealing with emails, along with a leaderboard system, to generate a safe and fun environment to teach the player how to deal with scenarios similar to real ones.

The player is faced with a sequence of levels, each with a list of emails they must classify according to the taught rules. Each level introduces new rules and emails become increasingly subtle, having a sense of progression and allowing them to reach the subtleness of their real life counterparts.

E-mails were composed of elements, the sender's name, e-mail address and picture, as well as the text content of the e-mail. Pointing out suspicious elments according to the criterea taught through the game results in higher scores at the end of the level.

To engage the players, the platform where we featured the game was built with a leaderboard. The sum of the highscore of each completed level resuletd on the value shown on the leaderbord, incentivizing players to perfect their skills on analyzing and spotting suspicious elements.
